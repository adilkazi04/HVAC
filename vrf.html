VRV / VRF system

VRV / VRF system

Our Excellent System Details

VRV stands for Variable Refrigerant volume and VRF stands for Variable refrigerant flow - Both are similar references to the HVAC technology used in air conditioning systems. While the company Daikin invented and registered VRV, the other companies use the term VRF to refer to the same technology used.

The goal of HVAC - heating, ventilation and air conditioning technology is to provide the best thermal comfort and good air quality in an indoor or vehicular environment.

Variable refrigerant flow is one of the most sophisticated technologies used in air conditioning systems. The VRF system installation circulates the minimum amount of refrigerant needed for a specific cooling/heating period which creates the possibility of users being able to control several air conditioning zones at one time.

The biggest advantage of the vrv system working principal is its ability to reduce power consumption and improve energy efficiency.

VRF system components helps to provide and control the refrigerant flow based on specific requirements of a building. A minimum amount of energy is used to maintain the temperature and automatically shuts off when there is no requirement for air conditioning. With controlled use, it helps to cut down the power costs for the users and reduce carbon emissions.

VRV / VRF system advantages is based on the simple principles that are briefed below

  • It uses the refrigerant as the only coolant material in the system
  • It uses the inverter compressors that allow lowering power consumption with partial cooling/heating heads
  • It has multiple indoor units on the same refrigerant loop

For example, there are 32 indoor air conditioning units connected to one outdoor unit. The VRF system functions as a multi-split unit. Each of the indoor units will have a different air conditioning requirement based on the temperature indoors and outdoors. Based on the total demand received from all the indoor units, the outdoor unit manages the refrigerant volume and temperature by providing only the necessary cooling or heating that is needed. Ultimately, this helps in a considerable amount of energy saving.

Under the HVAC system, The outdoor unit is constantly working to manage the compressor output based on the varying requirements received from the different indoor units. The system is fully automatic and regulates the power consumption based on the demands from indoor units and conditions prevailing outside.

Parameters associated with indoor units like whether the operation is on or off, type of operating state, specific temperature and fan speed are the aspects that need to be properly managed for proper operation. Once the systems are properly integrated with the VRF technology, the operation is usually seamless.

The ability to manage multiple units separately and account for externally prevalent conditions is what differentiates this new technology from the conventional approaches and makes it a widely accepted/technology in the current times.

Few examples of more advanced usage of the technology are in the home automation and building management systems for centralized monitoring and control. Management of the indoor units is also possible remotely over the internet via a simple and user-friendly interface.

The HVAC technology with the VRF systems diagram is of different types.

  • Cooling only systems - that provide only cooling and no heating is available.
  • Heating pump systems - the indoor units can support both cooling and heating, but one at a time.
  • Heat recovery systems - where heating and cooling are independently available for each indoor unit at the same time.
