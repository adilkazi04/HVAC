Many manufacturers collaborate with outsourced providers and sign an annual maintenance contract to overcome these challenges and ensure that maintenance is conducted as intended and necessary. An annual maintenance contract, also known as a contract maintenance agreement, is a business agreement between a manufacturing plant and a service provider for continuing maintenance. Unlike ad-hoc or emergency maintenance, industrial contract maintenance covers all areas of plant critical asset maintenance, including:

  • Checkups and tests on a regular basis
  • Repairs are required immediately.
  • A committed team of qualified and certified technicians
  • Process and documentation

Many manufacturing industries, particularly aerospace, automotive, construction products, heavy equipment, consumer packaged goods, paper/pulp, and tire/rubber, can benefit from an AMC (annual maintenance contract). The following are some of the advantages of a recurring annual maintenance service agreement: Building on the advantages of following a maintenance schedule, maintenance partners strive to reduce or eliminate downtime caused by equipment failures. An annual maintenance contract's long-term, holistic approach allows for downtime planning, ensuring that scheduled maintenance occurs at the least disruptive times in the production calendar.
