Chiller plants

Our Excellent System Details

A chiller plant is a centralized cooling plant with machinery to provide cooling for a large building, a combination of buildings and other big infrastructure setups. They are primarily used for air conditioning purposes and are also referred to as condensing units. The types in chiller plants are made of different mechanical equipment and work on the distribution of cool air throughout the buildings with the help of metal ducts through which the air is transported.

While HVAC systems are generally used in more confined spaces, chiller design are meant for bigger requirements and are more centralized. Chiller plant installation is meant to transfer the heat from water to a closed refrigerant system. The refrigerant is then pumped to the location from where it can be discharged to the atmosphere without any implications due to high temperature.

While the purpose of both air conditioners and chiller design calculation is to remove the heat, the working principle is slightly different. Air conditioners are used to regulate the temperature of a room. The heat from the air is transferred to water or another fluid and removed from the air that gets circulated producing the desired conditioning effect. In most systems, the principal refrigerant used is chilled water.

In the case of the chillers, the heat is removed from a liquid through vapour compression or absorption refrigeration cycle. The cooled liquid is sent through multiple pipes consisting of coils that help to dehumidify the air. Chiller-driven refrigerants are used for purposes like cooling of equipment and manufacturing processes. They are used to dehumidify air in large premises like hotels, factories and industrial spaces.

The two common types of chillers are

  • Air-cooled chiller that absorbs heat from the water and transfers it to the air in the atmosphere
  • Water-cooled chillers involve transferring chilled water through different pipes to cool machinery and other purposes.

The chiller design and control units are becoming more sophisticated and efficient with the use of new technologies. More efficiency is achieved by reducing the size of the plants and improving the distribution systems.

Since huge buildings depending on the chiller plants for their air conditioning requirements consume a lot of energy (that has been proven by statistical data and analysis by the concerned departments), the operational costs of the plants are extremely high as they require constant monitoring and maintenance. A large part of energy consumption by the buildings is considered to be due to the chiller plants.

Considering the energy aspect, optimized execution of the chiller plants is required and made possible with the help of chilled water pumps and chillers. One of the efficient techniques in chiller plants in India is the use of single water pumps for the distribution of cooling throughout the building. Another approach is the primary-secondary arrangement that covers the chillers and the buildings.

A quality control system is usually put in place to monitor the functioning of the chiller plants. With a good system in place, the equipment like chillers, pumps and other aspects can be controlled better and also the internal data can be analyzed further to identify any improvements.

Hiring a specialist or certified agency can be a recommended approach towards optimizing the efficiency of the chiller plants and related systems. By looking at the available data in detail, they should be able to suggest best practices and actions required to run the chiller plant in the best possible conditions.
