Cassette AC

Our Excellent System Details

Cassette air conditioning systems are models similar to the split air conditioning systems, the major difference being that cassette units are attached or fixed to the ceiling of the room or building and not to the wall as in the case of the split air conditioner. The cassette air conditioner also consists of two separate units with one fitted to the ceiling and the other installed outdoors.

The indoor unit of the cassette models is attached to the ceiling and distributes the conditioned air through three or four different sides of the unit. One advantage of the cassette models is that the airflow design allows cool air to sink and diffuse evenly thereby providing a uniform cooling experience for the entire room.

One of the models is the 4-way cassette ac which is unobtrusive and flexible. It fits well with the interior and is a good option for commercial use where the space is limited or rooms are smaller. 4-way cassette air conditioner being compact and easy to install, it should also fit well within the smaller spaces.

Yet another advantage of the cassette air conditioning units is that a major part of the equipment usually goes inside the ceiling and is hidden from outside view. Only the grills that are not thick show up outside and hence the unit happens to be less noticeable providing improved coverage for the entire space.

In comparison to the other types and models, cassette air conditioning units are used for larger spaces and often more powerful. The cost of the unit is also higher than the other variants.

Getting the units installed efficiently in large spaces and buildings can be a challenge. If the distance between the compressor and ceiling unit is quite large, it complicates the installation as multiple drilling may need to be done that can increase the cost and make it an expensive option. The installation of the complete unit can be a time-consuming one too. Being a costlier option compared to the split air conditioner, the cassette unit is probably not a good choice for smaller rooms and residential units. The bigger challenge associated with the installation also makes split air conditioners a more preferred option in such scenarios.

As these models are ceiling mounted, the selection of capacity also should be based on the size, height and other dimensions of the space. A space with a ceiling at a bigger height will require an air conditioner of bigger capacity.
