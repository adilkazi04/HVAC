Ductable AC

Our Excellent System Details

Ductable air conditioning systems are the models wherein the cooling or heating effect is provided through many ducts spread all over the home or other commercial spaces. The cool or hot air produced is distributed across space through the various ducts or vents thereby providing balanced effect and control.

The ductable ac specification are considered to be ideal for spaces like home, offices, schools, restaurants and other big spaces which require balanced air distribution across the room. A 3 ton ductable air conditioning unit is a model wherein the conditioned air is passed into the room through different ducts that are oval, rectangular or in other shapes. The ducts are made and fabricated using galvanized steel, fabric or pre-insulated boards. The installation of a ductable air conditioner is usually done on the roof or outside the building space. Return air from the room is passed back into the air conditioner. The space that needs to be air-conditioned is always kept airtight with the minimum circulation of fresh air gathered as per the ventilation standards to be followed.

It can be a good option when the requirement is to air condition multiple rooms or a big space. Instead of installing multiple air conditioning units across space, a single unit can be installed and air conditioning of all the rooms done using the ducts method (e.g. through the false ceiling) from the same unit. The ductable air conditioner can be a cost-saving option depending on the scenario.

Ducted units are more suitable for larger spaces as the temperature is better controlled. In the example of a house, if the requirement is to have air conditioning across all the rooms and cost is not an issue, the ducted air conditioning may be a better option. However, if the requirement is to have air conditioning in only a few of the rooms, ductable air conditioning is not a recommended option. As far as installation of ductable ac is concerned, the ducted air conditioning systems are more complicated and hence involve more cost. The system has multiple parts associated and hence would require a longer time to install the entire unit.

With the new sophisticated technology in the ducted air conditioning models, the space to be air-conditioned is divided into multiple zones and hence a more effective and efficient option compared to the old ducted systems. The zone-based techniques allow us to define which part or rooms of the space to be cooled instead of the entire big space thereby controlling the usage, energy consumption and saving cost too. A ducted air conditioning unit has a good life span depending on the usage and good maintenance. Usually, the systems are expected to last for a period of 15 to 20 years when managed well and used properly. Beyond a certain period, the parts of the unit get affected by wear and tear thereby reducing the reliability and efficiency.
